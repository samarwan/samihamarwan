Samiha Marwan

I am a PhD student in the college of Engineering, Department of Computer Science, at North Carolina State University. I am currently a Research Assistant in the HINTS lab supervised by Dr. Thomas Price. I LOVE teaching and I am so passionate about helping students learn how to code. I am currently working on developing support features in block-based programming environments, investigating their effect on students’ performance and understanding students’ perceptions on these support features. If you want to know more about my research projects, click on "Research tab" above.

I am originally from Egypt. I got my bachelor's degree in Bioinformatics, Faculty of Computer and Information Sciences in Ain Shams University in Egypt in 2012. I earned my Master's degree from the British University in Egypt in 2016 and my thesis topic was on DNA Steganography (don't be surprised :D, I know this is completely unrelated to my current work but I love both :)).
