My Research Work Projects

My research work focuses on creating automated help features for students while programming in Snap!, a block-based programming environment.

Next-step hints with textual explanations and self-explanations prompts

In this project I added textual explanations to next step hints, and self-explanation prompts to encourage students to self-reflect on the hints they receive. I found that students prefer having hints with textual explanations; and that having self-explanations prompts improved students' learning. The following is a demo on how this help feature looks like, and you can check my publications page to check published papers on this project.

Data-driven positive and corrective feedback

In this project I created data-driven positive and corrective immediate feedback through an objectives list that shows students their progress on subgoals. The objectives list provides students with progress bars on each subgoal using a hybrid data-driven subgoal detector model.
